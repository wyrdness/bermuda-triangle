## Notes on Sources

### Reliability Assessment

**High Reliability**:
- U.S. Navy and Coast Guard official reports
- NTSB aviation accident investigations
- Peer-reviewed scientific journals
- Lloyd's of London statistical data

**Medium Reliability**:
- Contemporary newspaper accounts (subject to sensationalism)
- Eyewitness testimony (subject to memory issues)
- Popular science documentaries (varying quality)

**Low Reliability**:
- Paranormal literature (often contains factual errors)
- Unverified internet sources
- Anecdotal accounts without corroboration
- Sensationalized media reports

### Known Errors in Popular Literature

Charles Berlitz's *The Bermuda Triangle* (1974) contains numerous factual errors documented by Larry Kusche:
- Ships that never existed
- Incidents that occurred outside the Triangle
- Missing vessels later found
- Weather conditions misrepresented
- Dates and locations incorrect

Researchers should **always verify** claims against primary sources.
